# Seed Starting & Plant Care Layers

## Quick Start: Transparent Pot Layers (Demo for Kids)

Perfect for teaching plant growth with visible soil layers:

1. **Drainage Layer** (bottom, ~1 inch): Small pebbles or broken terracotta
2. **Charcoal Layer** (~0.5 inch): Prevents odor & mold
3. **Potting Soil Mix** (~2-3 inches): Expert Gardener Indoor/Outdoor or similar
4. **Vermiculite Layer** (~0.5 inch): Moisture retention & aeration
5. **Seed Starting Mix** (top, ~1-2 inches): Jiffy Natural & Organic (lighter, better for germination)

Plant seeds at the top layer. Kids can watch roots grow down through layers!

## Seattle Seasonal Planting Guide

### Spring (March–May)
**Indoor Start (Feb–March for transplant by April)**
- Basil, tomatoes, peppers (warm season crops)
- Lettuce, spinach (direct sow outdoors late March)

**Outdoor Direct Sow (April–May)**
- Radish, peas, carrots, lettuce
- Sunflower (after last frost, ~mid-May)
- Chia microgreens (indoor on windowsill)

### Summer (June–August)
**Direct Sow Outdoors**
- Beans, squash, cucumber (after frost risk passes)
- Basil transplants from spring starts
- Marigold seeds (direct sow late May–June)

**Succession Planting**
- Plant lettuce/radish every 2 weeks for continuous harvest
- Keep watering consistent (Seattle: less rain in July–Aug)

### Fall (September–November)
**Indoor Start (August)**
- Fall crops: lettuce, spinach, kale (transplant Sept–Oct)
- Start chia for fall microgreens

**Direct Sow (September)**
- Cool-season seeds: carrots, beets, garlic cloves (plant Oct for spring harvest)
- Last basil cuttings before frost

### Winter (December–February)
**Indoor Only**
- Chia microgreens on windowsill (fastest, 7–10 days)
- Basil in grow lights or bright window
- Plan spring garden, order seeds

**Prep Work**
- Clean & sterilize pots
- Mix fresh potting soil
- Set up seed-starting station with lights

## Vermiculite

Vermiculite is a natural mineral that is heated to create a lightweight, porous material.
It is commonly used in gardening and horticulture for its ability to retain moisture and improve soil aeration.

### Uses in Gardening
- **Seed Starter Mix**: Add 20–30% vermiculite to potting mixes for optimal seedling growth
- **Potting Mixes & Garden Beds**: Enhance water retention and promote healthy root growth
- **Succulents & Orchids**: Serving both functional and decorative purposes as a versatile substrate
- **Vase Filler**: Commonly used as filler in vases and for surface covering in pots
- **Moisture Management**: Holds water without waterlogging; perfect for transparent pot layering

### Why Vermiculite Matters
- Porous structure supports root development
- Naturally pH neutral (safe for seedlings)
- Improves aeration while retaining moisture
- Visible through glass pots (educational for kids)

## Kid-Friendly Demo Setup

1. **Gather**: Transparent pot, pebbles, charcoal, potting soil, vermiculite, seed starter mix, chia seeds
2. **Layer together**: Let your kid place each layer, discussing what each does
3. **Plant & water**: Gently soak top layer; mist daily
4. **Observe**: Check daily for sprouting (chia: 3–7 days, very rewarding!)
5. **Harvest**: Eat chia microgreens in salads after 2 weeks

**Pro Tip**: Use a ruler to measure root growth; plot on a graph to track progress.
