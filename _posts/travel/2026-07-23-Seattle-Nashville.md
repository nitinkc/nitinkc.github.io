## The route

```
Seattle → Missoula → West Yellowstone (Yellowstone NP) → Salt Lake City →
Casper, WY → Mount Rushmore / Black Hills → Badlands NP → Sioux Falls →
Chicago → Nashville
```

This drops Denver entirely — it turned out to be a detour, not a shortcut. The direct Salt Lake City → Mount Rushmore run (via Rock Springs and Casper, WY) is actually **~255 miles shorter** than routing through Denver. Every day above is capped under 8 hours of driving, and the two biggest hauls (Badlands → Chicago, Chicago → Nashville) get their own dedicated days rather than being crammed onto the back of a park day.

## Day-by-day itinerary

### Day 1 — Seattle to Missoula, MT
**Drive:** ~7h 20min, 475 mi via I-90 E

Leave in the morning, not the evening — this is your longest single driving day, so treat it as a pure travel day. I-90 across Washington and the Idaho panhandle is easy, fast driving.

- **Stay:** Missoula — **La Quinta Inn & Suites Missoula** (~$110–130/night) or budget option **Motel 6 Missoula** (~$70–85/night). Downtown Missoula has good food if you arrive with energy left — Le Petit Outre or the Iron Horse Brew Pub.

---

### Day 2 — Missoula to West Yellowstone, MT
**Drive:** ~4h 20min, 265 mi via I-90 E then MT-287 S (through Butte and Ennis)

Short driving day on purpose — you'll arrive by early-to-mid afternoon with daylight to spare. This is your base for the next two nights, so no need to repack the car until Day 5.

- **Stay (3 nights):** West Yellowstone — **Explorer Cabins at Yellowstone** (~$150–190/night, nicer, worth it if you want real beds and a fireplace after two big driving days) or budget **Stage Coach Inn** (~$110–140/night) or **Yellowstone Moose Creek Cabins** (~$95–120/night). Book early — West Yellowstone fills up fast in summer.
- **Evening:** If you arrive with light left, the **Grizzly & Wolf Discovery Center** in town is a relaxed first taste of the park.

---

### Day 3 — Yellowstone: The Geyser Day (Lower Loop)
**Base:** West Yellowstone (no hotel change)

This is a sightseeing day, not a driving day — total driving inside the park is under 2 hours; everything else is boardwalks and overlooks.

- **Old Faithful + Upper Geyser Basin** (40–50 min from West Yellowstone) — allow 90 minutes minimum. Don't just watch Old Faithful; walk the basin for Castle, Grand, and Riverside Geysers.
- **Grand Prismatic Spring** — walk the **Overlook Trail** (~0.6 mi) for the full aerial view. Go before 9am or after 6pm to dodge the worst parking crush.
- **West Thumb Geyser Basin** (optional, evening) — geysers right on Yellowstone Lake's shore, usually quiet.

---

### Day 4 — Yellowstone: The Canyon Day (Upper Loop)
**Base:** West Yellowstone (no hotel change)

- **Norris Geyser Basin** (~1h from West Yellowstone) — the park's hottest, most unpredictable thermal area. Allow 60–90 minutes.
- **Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone + Artist Point** — the 308-ft Lower Falls in a 1,000-ft colored canyon. Allow 45–60 minutes.
- **Hayden Valley** — bison country between Canyon and the interior; you'll likely slow for a bison jam, which is part of the experience.
- **Optional, if you have energy:** push north to **Mammoth Hot Springs** (~1.5h from West Yellowstone) for the travertine terraces — this makes it a longer day (~5–6h round trip driving), so only do it if you're not feeling worn down from Day 3.

---

### Day 5 — West Yellowstone to Salt Lake City, UT
**Drive:** ~5h, 320 mi via US-20 S through Idaho Falls and Pocatello

A clean, moderate travel day. You'll arrive in Salt Lake City by mid-afternoon with the evening free.

- **Evening:** Walk **Temple Square** — it's free, well-lit at night, and an easy way to stretch your legs after driving.
- **Stay:** Salt Lake City — **Little America Hotel** (~$140–170/night, a genuinely nice downtown property, worth the splurge for one night before a long push east) or budget **Crystal Inn Downtown** (~$100–120/night).

---

### Day 6 — Salt Lake City to Casper, WY
**Morning:** Salt Lake City sightseeing (2–3 hours)
**Drive:** ~6h 30min, 412 mi via I-80 E then WY-220/US-26

Spend the morning in SLC before you go — **Temple Square** in daylight, the **Utah State Capitol** (free, great views), or a walk around **Liberty Park**. Then head east across southern Wyoming. This stretch is open, quiet high desert — fill up on gas before you leave SLC.

- **Stay:** Casper, WY — **Fairfield Inn & Suites Casper** (~$130–150/night) or budget **Motel 6 Casper** (~$75–90/night). Casper has a genuinely good downtown for a small city — check out **FireRock Steakhouse** or **Eggington's** for breakfast the next morning.

---

### Day 7 — Casper to Mount Rushmore / Rapid City, SD
**Drive:** ~3h 30min, ~225 mi via I-25 N then US-18/US-16 E

A short, easy driving day — you'll be checked into your hotel by early afternoon with plenty of time to see Rushmore itself in daylight and catch the evening lighting ceremony.

- **Afternoon:** Visit **Mount Rushmore National Memorial** — walk the Presidential Trail (easy, 0.6 mi loop) for the full view from below the faces.
- **Evening:** Stay for the **lighting ceremony** (nightly in summer, ~9pm) — it's genuinely worth staying for.
- **Stay:** Keystone or Rapid City, SD — **K Bar S Lodge** in Keystone (~$140–170/night, walkable to Rushmore, best location) or budget **Grand Gateway Hotel** in Rapid City (~$100–130/night).

---

### Day 8 — Black Hills Day → Badlands National Park
**Drive:** ~1h 45min total, 95 mi (Rushmore area → Badlands)

This is your fullest sightseeing day of the whole trip — Black Hills in the morning, Badlands by evening.

- **Morning:** **Custer State Park** — drive the **Wildlife Loop Road** (bison, pronghorn, burros) and **Needles Highway** (dramatic granite spires, tight tunnels — don't rush this).
- **Midday:** **Crazy Horse Memorial** — the mountain carving is still in progress; the visitor center and Native American cultural exhibits are worth an hour even if you don't linger long.
- **Late afternoon:** Drive to **Badlands National Park** (~1h 45min from the Rushmore area). Golden-hour light on the Badlands formations is spectacular — try to time your arrival for late afternoon.
- **Stay:** Wall, SD (right at the park's edge) — **Badlands Inn** (~$120–150/night, inside the park boundary, best sunrise access) or budget **Ranch Motel** in Wall (~$80–100/night). **Wall Drug** is a classic, slightly kitschy must-stop for dinner or a milkshake.

---

### Day 9 — Badlands Sightseeing → Sioux Falls, SD
**Morning:** Badlands sightseeing (2–3 hours)
**Drive:** ~5h 15min, ~350 mi via I-90 E

- **Morning:** Drive the **Badlands Loop Road (SD-240)** — stop at **Door**, **Window**, and **Notch Trail** (all short, under a mile). The eroded rock formations look like nothing else on this trip.
- **Drive east:** I-90 through the rest of South Dakota is flat and fast — a good day to just put on a podcast and cover ground.
- **Stay:** Sioux Falls, SD — **Hampton Inn Sioux Falls** (~$130–150/night) or budget **La Quinta Inn Sioux Falls** (~$90–110/night). If you have energy, **Falls Park** downtown (free, lit at night) is a nice quick stop.

---

### Day 10 — Sioux Falls to Chicago, IL
**Drive:** ~7h 45min, ~500 mi via I-90 E through Minnesota and Wisconsin

Your longest day since Day 1 — but still under 8 hours. Minnesota and Wisconsin farmland goes by quickly on I-90.

- **Stay (2 nights):** Chicago — budget-friendly options near the Loop or South Loop: **Freehand Chicago** (~$130–160/night, good design, central) or **Hampton Inn Chicago South Loop** (~$140–170/night). If you want to save more, hotels near O'Hare run ~$90–110/night but add a commute into downtown.

---

### Day 11 — Chicago: Full Sightseeing Day
**No driving today.**

A full rest-and-explore day before your final push to Nashville — you've earned it after the last three travel days.

- **Millennium Park** — Cloud Gate ("The Bean"), free and iconic.
- **Chicago Riverwalk + Architecture River Cruise** — genuinely one of the best ways to see the city; book ahead in summer.
- **Deep-dish pizza** — Lou Malnati's or Pequod's for the classic experience.
- **Navy Pier** in the evening if you want lake views and a Ferris wheel.

---

### Day 12 — Chicago to Nashville, TN
**Drive:** ~7h 10min, 472 mi via I-65 S through Indianapolis and Louisville

Your final day. I-65 is a straightforward, well-maintained interstate the whole way.

- **Optional stop:** Indianapolis (halfway point) for lunch, or Louisville if you want a longer break and a taste of Kentucky before Tennessee.
- **Arrival:** Welcome to Nashville — you're home.

---

## Buffer days

You've got 12 active days built into a 2-week window, which leaves **2 flexible days**. Good places to use them:
- An extra night in **Yellowstone** if weather or crowds slow you down (roads can still be affected by snow into early June).
- An extra night in **Chicago** if you want more time before the final push.
- A cushion at the **end**, arriving in Nashville with a day to spare before you need to be moved in — much better than arriving exhausted the same day you need to unload a truck.

---

## Budget notes

- **Where to spend a little more:** Little America in Salt Lake City and Explorer Cabins in West Yellowstone are worth the upgrade — both come after long driving stretches and set up the next day well.
- **Where to save:** Casper, Sioux Falls, and the O'Hare-area Chicago hotels are all solid budget picks without sacrificing much.
- **Fuel:** Roughly $450–550 total for the full one-way trip in a mid-size car averaging 28–30 mpg, at current gas prices.
- **Park fees:** $35 at Yellowstone's gate (7-day pass) covers you; Badlands is $30/vehicle (7-day) separately, or get the $80 **America the Beautiful annual pass** if you think you'll hit another national park within the year — it pays for itself after two parks.
